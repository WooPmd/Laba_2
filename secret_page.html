The Temple of Artemis was built in 560 BC. King Lydia Croesus in the city of Ephesus on the coast of Asia Minor. The huge white-marble temple was framed by 127 columns 18 meters high. Inside was a statue of Artemis, the goddess of fertility, made of gold and ivory. In 356 BC one vain inhabitant of Ephesus - Herostratus set fire to the temple, thus deciding to become famous and perpetuate his name. The sanctuary of Artemis was rebuilt, but in 263 it was destroyed and plundered by the Goths.
